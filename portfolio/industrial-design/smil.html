Project Description

Designed in 2008

SMIL, ‘System for Mobile & Informal Learning’, is a platform that enables highschool students to connect 'in-school' learning with 'out of school' discovering. This concept combines a personal device with modular sensors that a student can use to measure the world around him or her. The intuitive interface is designed to stimulate a project-based approach to challenges and develop an entrepreneurial mindset.

  • Device The touch-enabled device is designed have a long lasting battery-life, a robust shell and a low price point.
  • Sensors The pda has a built-in camera, microphone and motion sensor. Several external sensors (thermometer, magenetism, accelerometer, etc) can also be remotely connected to the device to conduct physical experiments. A student can then for example learn about gravity & acceleration laws of physics by going to a theme park and conduct experiments.

Design challenges

  • Understanding of learning processes
  • Low-budget public service
  • Hardware selection
  • Design of user interface

Key technologies

  • 4G LTE
  • Zigbee (sensors)

Key learning aspects

  • Validation product-market fit
  • User interface design
  • Product architecture & dataflows
